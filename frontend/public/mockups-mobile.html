Barrister Executive Brief

R v Johnson [2023] NSWCCA 45 • 23 Jan 2024

1. Case Overview

Sarah Johnson was convicted of murder on 12 March 2023 and sentenced to 30 years imprisonment with a 22-year non-parole period. Three viable grounds of appeal have been identified through analysis of the trial record, sentencing remarks, and relevant authorities.

2. Primary Ground: Misdirection on Intent

The strongest ground concerns the trial judge's failure to give an adequate Andar direction. The summing-up at pp 214-218 conflated intention and recklessness despite specific defence request. Supported by R v Andar [2015] HCA 40.

3. Sentencing Disparity

Comparative analysis of 15 NSW cases reveals the 22-year NPP falls significantly outside the established range of 14-18 years. R v Loveridge [2014] NSWCCA 120 provides direct authority.

4. Recommended Strategy

Lead with Ground 1 (misdirection) as the primary ground, supported by Ground 3 (manifestly excessive sentence). Ground 2 (unreasonable verdict) should be preserved but presents a higher burden.
