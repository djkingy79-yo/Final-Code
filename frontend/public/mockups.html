R v Johnson [2023] NSWCCA 45 • Generated 23 Jan 2024

1. Case Overview

The appellant, Sarah Johnson, was convicted of murder on 12 March 2023 and sentenced to 30 years imprisonment with a non-parole period of 22 years. The conviction arose from the death of the deceased on 15 August 2022. Three viable grounds of appeal have been identified through comprehensive analysis of the trial record, sentencing remarks, and relevant authorities.

2. Primary Ground: Misdirection on Intent

The strongest ground concerns the trial judge's failure to give an adequate Andar direction on the distinction between intention and recklessness. The summing-up at pp 214-218 conflated these elements despite specific defence request. This ground has strong prospects, supported by R v Andar [2015] HCA 40 and the specific deficiency identified in the trial transcript.

3. Sentencing Disparity

Comparative analysis of 15 comparable NSW cases reveals the 22-year NPP falls significantly outside the established range of 14-18 years for similar offences. R v Loveridge [2014] NSWCCA 120 and R v Pham [2015] HCA 39 provide direct authority for this submission.

4. Recommended Strategy

Counsel is advised to lead with Ground 1 (misdirection) as the primary ground, supported by Ground 3 (manifestly excessive sentence) as an alternative. Ground 2 (unreasonable verdict) should be preserved but presents a higher evidentiary burden.
